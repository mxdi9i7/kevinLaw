What we do

Are you experiencing immigration problems? Have you been charged with a crime? Have you been injured? Getting a divorce? King L. Wu & Associates speaks your language and our attorneys have experience you can trust.

Asylum

How can you explain to the U.S. government that you left your country because you feared for your safety and your freedom? The process of applying for asylum is very complex – even for English speakers. You will need help from an experienced immigration attorney to increase your chances for a successful asylum application.

Family Petition

As a U.S. citizen or Lawful Permanent Resident (LPR), you can help a “qualifying relative” obtain a green card. In order to do so, you must first provide sufficient documents to verify your status as a U.S. citizen or LPR.

Removal Defense

Perhaps no aspect of the immigration process is more fearsome, complex and fraught with risk as deportation. Successful removal defense requires help from an experienced immigration attorney. Don’t hesitate to consult one of our immigration attorneys if you believe you could be facing deportation.

H-1B

H-1B is used for primarily for immigrants with “specialty occupations.” In general, to qualify for a specialty occupation visa, an individual must have at least a Bachelor Degree or a combination of education and work experience in the specialty occupation can be considered equivalent to a degree.

Investment immigration (EB-5)

The EB-5 program was created in order to encourage foreign investors to make capital investments and help fuel the U.S. economy through job growth. This program allows investors who financially have the means to invest in the American economy to obtain a green card. All investors that are interested in an EB-5 visa must invest in a new commercial enterprise.

Naturalization

If your goal is to become a legal resident or a citizen, the attorneys of King L. Wu & Associates have the knowledge, experience and language skills to take you through the process, overcome any obstacles and achieve your goal.

Temporary visas

If you are a non-U.S. citizen or permanent resident who has considered visiting the U.S. for a set period of time, it is in your best interests to speak with an attorney in regards to obtaining a short-term visa, also known as a non-immigrant visa.

Appeals and Motions

Immigration procedures are extremely complex and often unpredictable. Despite one’s best efforts to advance a case in a U.S. immigration agency, the initial results can often be disappointing.

DACA and SIJS

Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) is an American immigration policy that allows certain undocumented immigrants to the United States who entered the country before their 16th birthday and before June 2007 to receive a renewable two-year work permit and exemption from deportation.

Lawful Permanent Resident Status (“Green Card”)

A Green Card shows that you have lawful permanent residency in the U.S., meaning that you have rights and privileges second only to those of U.S. citizens.
